Research

Single-molecule views of nucleic acid mechanics and interactions

The lab combines physical measurements, biochemical assays, and simulations to understand DNA, RNA, RNA-DNA hybrids, and mechanically active biomolecular systems.

Themes

Research areas

Nucleic acid mechanics

Mechanical properties of DNA, RNA, and RNA-DNA hybrids under force and torque.

DNA and RNA elasticity

Elastic responses that connect molecular sequence, structure, and environmental conditions.

RNA-DNA hybrid mechanics

Physical behavior of hybrid duplexes relevant to transcription, replication, and genome regulation.

DNA topology and knots

Topological states of DNA and their consequences for molecular processes.

Protein-DNA interactions

How proteins recognize, bind, deform, and regulate DNA at the single-molecule level.

Nucleic acid stability

Ion-mediated stability and the effects of epigenetic DNA modifications on nucleic acid behavior.

Methods

Integrated experimental and computational toolkit

Single-molecule magnetic tweezers

Force and torque spectroscopy for probing molecular mechanics and topology.

Single-molecule fluorescence

Observation of molecular states, dynamics, and interactions in individual molecules.

Biochemical assays

Ensemble measurements that complement and validate single-molecule observations.

Molecular dynamics simulations

Computational models that connect experimental observables with molecular mechanisms.

Current Directions

From nucleic acid physics to biological function

Epigenetic regulation

Single-molecule measurements of how DNA modifications alter elasticity, stability, structural dynamics, and protein-mediated processes.

RNA structure and modification

Quantitative studies of RNA folding, duplex mechanics, modified nucleotides, and RNA-DNA hybrid behavior.

DNA force probes

Programmable DNA-based probes for measuring molecular forces in living cells and connecting force dynamics with cell behavior.
