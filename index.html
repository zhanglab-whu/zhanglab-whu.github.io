College of Life Sciences, Wuhan University

Develop new single-molecule technologies, design DNA mechanical probes for cell mechanobiology and clinical sample detection, reveal how methylation regulates DNA structure and function, and uncover dynamic mechanisms of nucleic-acid structural regulation.

Zhang Lab studies the single-molecule biophysics of DNA, RNA, and RNA-DNA hybrids, connecting molecular mechanics with genome stability, epigenetic regulation, clinical genotyping, and cell mechanobiology.

Research Interests

Single-molecule techniques and nucleic-acid structural dynamics

Single-molecule techniques

Development of magnetic tweezers, fluorescence probes, and force-based assays for quantitative molecular biology.

DNA and RNA dynamics

Structural and mechanical regulation of DNA, RNA, RNA-DNA hybrids, and modified nucleic acids.

Clinical genotyping

DNA probe design for mutation-sensitive, multiplexed, amplification-free detection of nucleic acids.

Cell mechanobiology

DNA-based mechanical probes for measuring force magnitude, duration, and loading rate in living cells.

Genome stability

Epigenetic regulation of DNA mechanics, protein-DNA interactions, and DNA repair processes.

Methods

Experimental and computational toolkit

Magnetic tweezers

Single-molecule force and torque measurements of nucleic acids and protein-nucleic acid complexes.

Fluorescence

Single-molecule fluorescence assays for conformational dynamics, force probes, and molecular interactions.

Biochemical assays

Ensemble and functional assays that connect single-molecule observations to biological mechanisms.

Molecular dynamics

Simulations that interpret measurements and test mechanistic models of nucleic-acid structure and mechanics.

Research Areas

Mechanics, topology, and stability of nucleic acids

Research in the lab spans nucleic acid mechanics, DNA and RNA elasticity, RNA-DNA hybrid mechanics, DNA topology and knots, protein-DNA interactions, ion-mediated nucleic acid stability, and epigenetic DNA modifications.

Principal Investigator

Prof. Xing-Hua Zhang

Prof. Xing-Hua Zhang is a professor in the College of Life Sciences at Wuhan University. His research focuses on single-molecule techniques, DNA and RNA structural dynamics, clinical genotyping, cell mechanobiology, and genome stability under epigenetic regulation.
