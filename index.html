College of Life Sciences, Wuhan University

Single-molecule biophysics of DNA, RNA, and RNA-DNA hybrids

Zhang Lab develops and applies single-molecule methods to understand nucleic acid mechanics, structural dynamics, molecular interactions, and force-regulated biological processes.

Research Areas Contact the Lab

Approach

Quantitative molecular biophysics

Magnetic tweezers

Single-molecule force and torque measurements of nucleic acids and protein-nucleic acid complexes.

Fluorescence

Single-molecule fluorescence assays for conformational dynamics and molecular interactions.

Biochemistry

Complementary biochemical assays to connect molecular mechanisms with ensemble behavior.

Simulations

Molecular dynamics simulations to interpret experiments and test mechanistic models.

Research

Mechanics, topology, and stability of nucleic acids

Research in the lab spans nucleic acid mechanics, DNA and RNA elasticity, RNA-DNA hybrid mechanics, DNA topology and knots, protein-DNA interactions, ion-mediated nucleic acid stability, and epigenetic DNA modifications.

Principal Investigator

Prof. Xing-Hua Zhang

Prof. Xing-Hua Zhang is a professor in the College of Life Sciences at Wuhan University. His research focuses on single-molecule techniques, DNA and RNA structural dynamics, clinical genotyping, cell mechanobiology, and genome stability under epigenetic regulation.
